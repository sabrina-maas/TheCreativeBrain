Interactive Brain




Neuroimaging

MRI



MRI (magnetic resonance imaging) scans use magnetic fields and radio waves to produce computer-generated images of soft-tissue. They show brain anatomy.

fMRI (functional MRI) scans reveal bloodflow, and therefore brain activity, by comparing successive MRI scans. They show brain function as well as structure and reveal how labor is divided in the brain.

CT



CT (computed tomography) scans are a series of x-ray photographs taken from different angles and combined by a computer into a complete represetation of a slice of the structure of the brain. They are also known as CAT scans.
